Research

Active programs & projects

We combine rigorous mathematical foundations with hardware validation in laboratory and field environments, advancing the frontier of autonomy in space exploration.

Multi-Agent Autonomy

Coordinated Formation Sensing (CADRE)

CADRE (Cooperative Autonomous Distributed Robotic Exploration) is a lunar technology demonstration of multi-agent autonomy on a team of three rovers and a base station. Receiving only high-level tasks from Earth, the rovers autonomously explore a region of the lunar surface and perform a distributed measurement in coordination with a multi-static ground-penetrating radar.

Multi-Modal Mobility

EELS — Exobiology Extant Life Surveyor

Ice worlds are at the forefront of astrobiological interest because of evidence of subsurface oceans. To sample Enceladus' vent systems, NASA JPL is developing a snake-like robot, EELS, that navigates extreme surfaces and descends erupting vents to capture unaltered liquid samples — potentially reaching the ocean. We contribute dynamics modeling and resilient autonomy for such platforms.

Introspective Control

Learning-Enabled Introspective Neural Control

The DARPA Learning Introspective Control (LINC) program develops machine-learning-based introspection that enables physical systems — ground vehicles, ships, drone swarms, robots — to respond to events not predicted at design time. LINC updates control laws in real time while providing guidance and situational awareness to the operator, human or autonomous.

On-Orbit · ISAM

Collaborative On-Orbit Inspection

Inspection and mapping of a target spacecraft in low Earth orbit using multiple observer spacecraft in stable passive relative orbits is a key enabling technology for future missions. Our guidance and control architecture uses an information-gain approach to directly trade off gathered data against fuel and energy cost.

Stochastic Optimal Control

Trajectory Optimization of Chance-Constrained Nonlinear Stochastic Systems

We developed generalized polynomial chaos-based sequential convex programming (gPC-SCP) to compute suboptimal solutions for continuous-time chance-constrained stochastic nonlinear optimal control problems, enabling motion planning under uncertainty. The method first derives a deterministic surrogate with convex constraints via gPC expansion and a distributionally robust convex subset of the chance constraints, then solves it using sequential convex programming for trajectory generation and control.
