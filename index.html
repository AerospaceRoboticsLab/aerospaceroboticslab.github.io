Mission

Intelligent decisions under uncertainty

At the heart of our work is one question: how can robotic systems make intelligent decisions in the face of uncertainty, while ensuring safety, robustness, and mission success? We formulate and solve information-cost stochastic optimal control problems, drawing on nonlinear control, distributional robustness, convex optimization, and probability theory.

Research thrusts

Four directions, one frontier

Extreme Terrain

Multi-Modal Robotic Systems

We design and prototype multi-modal robots that traverse highly variable environments — planetary caves, ice crevasses, steep slopes. Inspired by platforms like EELS, they combine wheeled, legged, and slithering locomotion with onboard autonomy: dynamics modeling, real-time control adaptation, and perception-informed motion planning.

Decision-Making

Safe Exploration Under Uncertainty

We develop scalable methods for perception-driven planning and control under bounded and unbounded uncertainty, using distributionally robust optimization, uncertainty quantification, and probabilistic inference.

Multi-Agent

Collaborative Autonomy

We study how heterogeneous teams of robots — rovers, drones, and free-flyers — reason jointly, share information, and act cooperatively under resource, communication, and safety constraints: distributed task allocation, formation planning, and multi-agent motion planning with limited observability.

In-Space · ISAM

On-Orbit Inspection & Assembly

We enable autonomous in-space assembly and reconfiguration of large structures such as antennas and trusses — multi-spacecraft coordination, contact dynamics, and uncertainty-aware manipulation for next-generation orbital construction.

Concepts in motion

The ideas behind our work, animated

Stochastic Optimal Control

Chance-constrained planning

An agent tracks the optimal path while a bounded uncertainty ellipse grows and shrinks; sampled trajectories stay inside a corridor that keeps the obstacle clear with high probability.

Multi-Agent Autonomy

Coordinated sensing

Three observer agents follow stable passive relative orbits around a target, sharing information to jointly inspect it under fuel and communication constraints.

Lunar Exploration

Rovers on the Moon

Autonomous rovers traverse uncertain lunar terrain, leaving tracks as they map the surface and search for resources ahead of crewed missions.

Director / Principal Investigator

Assistant Professor · Daniel Guggenheim School of Aerospace Engineering

Dr. Nakka leads the Aerospace Robotics Lab at Georgia Tech. His long-term vision is to enable trustworthy, adaptive, and resilient robotic systems that serve as partners in exploration, science, and infrastructure development in the most challenging environments known to humanity.
